Vocabulary Book

A classic "work smarter, not harder" solution to expanding your vocabulary

Why should I make a vocab book?

  • Do you forget words all the time?
  • Are you tired of having to rely too much on Google Translate or Word Reference?
  • Is your limited vocabulary book impeding on your reading, writing, listening, and speaking?
  • Or do you just want to impress your friends by knowing more than they do?

Congratulations! I have a solution for you:

The vocab book

What's a vocab book?

It's a convenient way for you to retain all of those fancy new words you've been trying so hard to collect, all inside of a book - kind of like storing pokeballs in a pokedex so you have all your pokemon in one neat and tidy place.

Who uses a vocab book?

Succesful students with impressive vocabularies -that's who!

How do I make a vocab book?

It's not as hard as you think. Just follow these steps:

  1. Grab a small notebook like this.
  2. Whenever you see a word you do not know, write it down in your notebook. If you do not have your notebook with you, write it down somewhere else then enter it in later.
  3. Write down the translation in your language next to the word, or in a column next to your column of words, or on the next page. Whatever works for you.
  4. It is also helpful to write the definition of the word in English, or use the word in a sentence, or both.

That's it - you're done!

Who knows why so many students never bother doing this simple, 2 minute task that will actually save them much more time in the long run (successful students will vouch for the vocab book's worth).

Don't forget to look through your vocab book periodically. Just flip through the pages browsing effortlessly out of boredom or curiosity.

One last thing: your vocab book can do much more than single words: try adding expressions or phrasal verbs

If you are taking an exam, I highly reccomend you download and/or print that exam's vocabulary list. (since 50% of you are PET students: here's a link) Divide the list up and look through it with your vocab book.
